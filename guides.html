ℹ️ About / Information

The Ringvold instructional shift session outlines the steps necessary for setting up, conducting, and concluding a shift at our facility. By following these detailed instructions, our staff will maintain high standards of operation and ensure a smooth and effective shift, aligning with our facility’s protocols and expectations.

⚠️ Session Startup Instructions

Before initiating any shift, the following procedures must be completed in full. Proper preparation is critical for a successful session, organized structure, and recognized hosting credit:

  • You must have a minimum of five (5) attendees to commence the shift.
  • Selecting hardest workers is optional, not mandatory. If you choose to appoint them, you may select up to four (4) per session only.
  • A co-host is optional, but if selected, they must hold the rank of Superintendent or higher. You may only have one (1) co-host at maximum.
  • Sessions that fail to meet requirements or collapse will not count toward quota. Prepare thoroughly, and review session guidelines prior to hosting.
  • Have 1 (maximum) session supervisor. Go to our discord and request a supervisor in the 🚦 | supervisor-requests channel using the correct format.

📢 Announce the Session

Broadcast the following command in-game to notify the server:

":h There's a Shift being hosted in the session room, come to the session room and STS to show activity and possibly receive a new rank!"

⚙️ Session Management Requirements

The following requirements outline the standards all hosts must follow when managing active sessions. Adhering to these ensures consistency, fairness, and smooth operational flow throughout the shift:

Management Professionalism:

Session administrators must remain professional at all times. The way you conduct yourself sets the tone for the rest of the session.

Management Grammar:

Must use proper grammar at ALL times. Clear and correct communication is essential. Do not remove attendees for lack of grammar, but offer corrective recommendations to them.

Management Respect:

Management members must respect ALL attendees. A respectful environment fosters better participation and engagement.

Management Rule Enforcement:

Remove any rule breakers from the shift promptly to maintain order and focus.

Chairperson Supervision & Requirements:

Members ranked Chairperson must have a supervisor present. Supervisors are required to ensure the shift meets organizational standards.

Facility Director(s) may supervise Chairperson’s but must have a Community Advisory Council+ to log possible promotions. However, if a Facility Director has Evaluated High Rank permissions, they may supervise without a Community Advisory Council.

💡 Shift Preparation Tips

Before initiating any shift, hosts should review the following preparation points to maintain control, ensure productivity, and deliver a well-structured experience for all participants.

  • REVIEW SHIFT PROTOCOLS: Before starting the shift, thoroughly review all shift protocols and procedures. Being well-versed in these will help you manage the shift effectively and handle any issues that arise.
  • PREPARE FOR COMMON SCENARIOS: Anticipate common scenarios or problems that might occur during the shift and be ready with solutions. This will help ensure the shift runs smoothly.
  • SET CLEAR EXPECTATIONS: Clearly outline the goals and expectations for the shift at the beginning. This helps attendees understand their roles and responsibilities throughout the shift.
  • ENGAGE YOUR ATTENDEES: Encourage active participation from all attendees. Use directives and prompts to keep everyone engaged and ensure they understand their roles.
  • COLLECT FEEDBACK: Gather feedback from attendees at the end of the shift. This can provide valuable insights into what went well and what could be improved for future shifts.

🎬 Session Introduction

This section is designed to set the tone for the shift by introducing the hosts and outlining key rules and expectations. It ensures all participants understand the importance of respect, proper conduct, and active participation.

STAND SHOULDER TO SHOULDER ON THE HOST/CO-HOST INSTRUCTIONAL LINE

Host

" Hello. Welcome to today’s shift! My name is [Name], and I’ll be your host. "

Co-Host

" Hey! I’m [Name], your co-host, and I’ll be assisting in today’s shift. "

Host

" Before we begin, let’s go over a few rules to help you succeed in this shift! "

" I - Do not say PTS (Permission To Speak) while I am speaking. "
" II - Follow all orders given to you by a SI+ unless they ask you to break the rules. "
" III - If there is any problem, come to me, my Co-Host, or any other SI+ for assistance. "
" IV - Do not ask for promotions/ranks. "
" V - Use proper grammar at all times. "

📋 Session Details & Policies

To ensure a smooth and effective shift, hosts and co-hosts should adhere to the following policies:

  • Active Communication: Hosts and co-hosts must consistently monitor attendees and maintain communication via radio throughout the patrol portion of the shift.
  • Captain Selection: Only one captain is permitted per shift. A Warden or higher may be designated as Captain.
  • Role Assignment: The host assigns roles individually to all attendees and provides explanations once roles are assigned. Ensure every participant fully understands their responsibilities before starting.
  • Role Confirmation: Verify that each attendee comprehends their assigned role and duties. Once confirmed, attendees may begin their tasks.
  • Shift Duration: Shifts are expected to last 25–35 minutes, with a maximum of 40 minutes. Shifts exceeding this time are not permitted.

🛡️ Shift Roles

The host or co-host will assign roles to all attendees. Each participant is expected to patrol the prison according to their assigned responsibilities.

Host

" Before we start, my co-host will assign your roles. Please pay close attention, as your role will dictate your duties for the duration of this shift. "

" Does everyone have a role? (Yes/No, <Rank>). "

" No responses will be taken as a yes. "

Now: Wait at max: 10 seconds. Then move on. If an attendee didn't get a role; assign them a role. If an attendee said yes: Don't approach them and carry on with these texts below:

Host

" Now that everyone has their roles, let’s review what each role entails: "

  • Patrol Team: Responsible for patrolling the prison and monitoring its status. They ensure that all areas are secure and operational.
  • Scout Team: Patrols outside the prison and monitors the premises from watch towers, providing an external security layer.
  • Response Team: Handles situations around the set status, responds to any panic calls, and oversees checkpoints and the control room.
  • Camera Operator: Manages the cameras, providing real-time updates and reporting on prison activities via radio.
  • Captain: Leads a group, maintains control within their team, and ensures order and discipline are upheld.

🚀 Shift Dismissal & Drill Rules

This section provides instructions for sending attendees to their assigned posts at the start of a shift.

Host

" At a random point during the shift, a drill will be conducted. It is crucial that you follow the procedures specific to the drill being carried out. "

" Please pay close attention as I explain the procedures for each drill: "

Lockdown Drill: " When a Lockdown Drill is initiated, all attendees must escort inmates back to their cells. Ensure that all individuals remain within the cell-block for the duration of the drill. "

Blackout Drill: " When a Blackout Drill is initiated, all facility power will be shut off. Guards and inmates must remain inside the cell-block for the entire duration of the drill. Do not attempt to restore power until the procedure has officially concluded. "

Evacuation Drill: " When the Evacuation Drill starts, all attendees and inmates must proceed to the Yard and stand there. You must evacuate as soon as the alarm commences. Do not transfer inmates. "

" Before I dismiss you to your duties, here are a few expectations that we expect throughout the shift. "

" I - Communication: Use radio channel two to communicate. "
" II - Radio Format: When communicating on the radio, follow this format: [Role] (Text). For example: [Patrol] Cell Block is clear! "
" III - Door Management: Please remember NOT to leave doors open and NOT to use lethal force unnecessarily. "
" IV - Adherence: Maintain clear communication and follow the instructions of the host and co-host at all times. "
" V - Community Guidelines: Follow ROBLOX's ToS and refrain from breaking the rules. You are expected to understand and abide to ROBLOX's ToS and Ringvold's Community Guidelines. "

🔒 Lockdown Drill (Optional)

Designed to practice responding to a security alert within the facility. Control panel abuse regulations are strictly enforced.

Host/Co-Host: “:h We are initiating a Lockdown. All inmates must be escorted back to their cells immediately. During this lockdown, cuff abuse will not be noted. Do not enter the control-room unless told by a session admin.”

ENFORCE CONTROL PANEL ABUSE PENALTIES FOR ANY VIOLATIONS OF THIS RULE. OBSERVE AND NOTE ACTIVE INVOLVEMENT, GOOD COMMUNICATION, AND INMATE MANAGEMENT.

Host/Co-Host/Supervisor: “:h Please do not alter the Status or tamper with the Lockdown settings during the session. Unauthorized changes will result in a punishment.”
Host/Co-Host/Supervisor: “:h The situation has been lifted. Facility operations have been resumed.”

🌑 Blackout Drill (Optional)

Practice exercise for a facility-wide power outage. Note that radio communication will be completely unavailable during this drill. Co-hosts should remain near the power box to prevent tampering. Wait two minutes before restoring power.

Host/Co-Host: “:h We are experiencing an active Blackout. All inmates must be escorted to the cell-block immediately. Guards and staff are to stay within the cell-block as well. No personnel should be in the hallways during this blackout.”

ENFORCE CONTROL PANEL ABUSE PENALTIES FOR ANY VIOLATIONS. OBSERVE AND NOTE ACTIVE INVOLVEMENT AND LEADERSHIP EFFECTIVENESS.

Host/Co-Host/Supervisor: “:h The situation has been resolved. Facility operations have been resumed.”

🚨 Evacuation Drill (Optional)

Moves all staff and inmates to the Yard Checkpoint Wall under a 200-second timeline. Requires at least 6 attendees and 4 inmates present (otherwise, obtain approval from Board of Governors+ or Overseer Command).

ANNOUNCE THREE SEPARATE TIMES TO ENSURE ALL ATTENDEES ARE INFORMED.

Host/Supervisor: “:h An evacuation is now in effect. Proceed to the Maximum Security Yard. All personnel must be at the checkpoint to comply with roleplay. Evacuate promptly and leave all prison doors open. Do not raid.”

ENFORCE CONTROL PANEL ABUSE PENALTIES FOR ANY VIOLATIONS.

Host/Co-Host: “:h The situation has been resolved. Facility operations have been resumed.”

🏁 Session Conclusion & Evaluation

Bring the shift to a structured close, log parameters, and evaluate rewards:

Host: “:h Can everyone from the shift please report back to the session room. Call (X/2)”
  • Mark Attendance: Tick “passed” on notepad (!notepad) for attendees who successfully completed the shift phases.
  • Promote: Confirm passers and proceed to ranking. Passers need background stats and must be off cooldown.
  • Grade Staff: Session administration should use the !notepad command to grade each other's performance.
  • Logging: Submit the logs using the correct format in the official Discord communications server channels.

ONCE ALL ATTENDEES ARE BACK, SET THEM TO STS AGAIN AND TAKE THE SCREENSHOT FOR THE SHIFT LOG.

Host

" Welcome back, everyone! Before we announce the hardest workers, we’ll take a group photo. Please remain in STS formation during this time. "

" Now, let’s recognize the hardest workers. Everyone did a great job today, but these attendees stood out for their exceptional performance: [ANNOUNCE THE HARDEST WORKERS...] "

" Thank you all for attending today’s shift. It’s been a pleasure being your host! "

Co-Host

" Thank you for participating. You are all now dismissed! "

Host/Co-Host/Supervisor: “:h The shift is now officially over! Passers, please type !rejoin to view your new ranks. All others are dismissed from the session room.”
